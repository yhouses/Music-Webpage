Woodstock Music and Art Fair


Music has been a monument in human history. It has been a way to release messages and make out of the ordinary sound that can inspire hundreds. Music has always been a way for people to liberate themselves and it has worked it's way into political, social, as well as religious clauses. With this new infatuation of this current generation of resurrecting some classics, it's important we acknowledge the power of music, which is what John Roberts, Joel Rosenman, Artie Kornfield, and Michael Lang wanted to do. With all the chaos occurring at that time, they just wanted "three days of peace and music". They also wanted to raise enough money from this event to construct a recording studio in the town of Woodstock.

This festival was a huge success since about 400,000 people attended this festival and it is still continued today. Musical masterpieces were created at the festival and it had a huge elevation in the rates of rock lovers. This festival is one of the most pivotal moments in music history. Though many famous artists and bands did not participate in this monumental festival, the lineup was still incredible, especially with Jimi Hendrix closing the festival.

The Performance That Made History

Jimi Hendrix was the closing headline of the Woodstock Music and Art Fair. Unfortunately, due to unpredicted rains that occurred the night before, many of the festival participants headed back to their previous locations. Only a percentage of the original audience stayed for the unforgettable performance that was greatly delivered. In this time, the Vietnam War was happening, which spiked the hippie era, in which people were advocating for peace and the return of our USA troops. Hendrix decided to incorporate this controversial message into his performance. Thus, the big uproar it received. With his own rendition of the Star-spangled banner, he created a clear visual all with the help of his guitar. With this traditional, patriarchal tune that has been idolized in the minds of many, he showed the pain, fear, and violence that this war was causing. He spoke for many with this performance and in this performance, was he labeled as 'the greatest guitarist player to exist'.
